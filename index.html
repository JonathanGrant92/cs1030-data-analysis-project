    Analysis

    Statistics

    The average of median area income is $68,110. The fair market rent average is $1,164. The average poverty income level is $15,987. The average annual household income is $61,536. The average monthly housing cost is $1061.61 or $12,739.36 annually. Note that the housing cost is approximately 79.69% of the average poverty income level.
    Burden is a housing cost metric determined by the division of monthly housing cost over monthly income for a record (HADS: 5).

    Renter Types

    Approximate 55% of records indicate home ownership. The remaining records indicate renters of three categories of units; apartments, houses, and not specified.

    Burden to Income Correlation?

    Monthly household income is negatively correlated to the housing cost burden of households. The relationship approximates the positive x-axis of the parent function x^-2. The housing cost burden is the quadratic reciprocal of your household income. The less income you earn is quadratically related to your housing burden; burden increase steepens with less income.

    Conclusions

    The analysis and visuals of burden cost over monthly income answers the first inquiry. There exists a strong negative correlation between housing cost burden and a household's income. So, households below the poverty line carry a disproportionate share of the nation's housing burden.
